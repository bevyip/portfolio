Beverly Yip

Developer | Innovator | Designer

Developer

I began coding my freshman year of college in National University of Singapore as a Math major. I then realized the endless possibilities and significance behind the innovations I could create by being a programmer. Thus, I decided to transfer to Purdue University and now major in Computer Science. Since then, I have learned a variety of languages and enjoyed it ever since.

Designer

Since young, I’ve always had an eye for design and an interest in art. When I discovered the possibilities from combining programming and visual art, I realized the importance of user-empathy and design for the front-end aspects of every application. I currently also engage in creating content for the marketing departments for several organizations on campus and for my Instagram for companies around the world.

Innovator

After becoming a Computer Science major, I've begun to enjoy producing side programming projects in my free time. Ideally, I create to benefit as much of society as possible and to make an impactful difference to those in need.

Leader/ Teacher

I am an individual who pushes herself out of her comfort zone to explore and learn every day. In the past few years, I’ve taken up minor and majors roles in several minority organizations on campus and love to meet individuals from all walks of life. This is why I also pursue a minor in Anthropology, a way to understand cultures other than my own. I love to also share my knowledge and passions with others, which is why I also serve as a Teaching Assistant in the CS180 course and a mentor in many CS minority programs.
