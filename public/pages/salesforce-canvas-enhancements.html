Developer

I first ventured into coding during my freshman year at the National University of Singapore while majoring in Math. It soon dawned on me the vast potential and profound impact of the innovations possible through programming. This realization led me to transfer to Purdue University in 2016, where I pursued a major in Computer Science with a specialization in Machine Learning. Occasionally, I dabble in side programming projects, seeking inventive and user-centric solutions to real-world challenges. Since starting my journey in CS, I have learned a variety of programming languages and enjoy developing projects.

Designer

From a young age, I've been drawn to design and captivated by art. My fascination grew when I realized the blend of programming with design, prompting my dive into Computer Science. There, I learned and embodied the importance of an empathetic, research-based, and human-centered approach to technical solutions. I was actively engaged in designing content for the marketing departments of several campus organizations, pursued a minor in Anthropology to delve deeper into human interactions, and consistently prioritized the end-user in my solutions as a Software Engineer.

Collaborator

I've consistently valued the power and advantages of collaboration and the insights that come from constructive feedback. While at Salesforce, I was involved in different aspects of designing and developing solutions for our users. Collaborating with UX teams and Product Managers, I brainstormed ideas, undertook research, documented findings, and coordinated tasks with my engineering peers. Additionally, I partnered with parallel teams to discuss accessibility shortcomings and solutions in our products. With every solution, I intend on designing and engineer with growing empathy and intuition for the end user - an endeavor made achievable through ongoing teamwork and open communication.

Leader

While at Purdue, I took on minor and major roles in several minority organizations. One noticeable leadership role was my tenure as the President of the Society of Asian Scientists and Engineers (SASE). With the support of my leadership team, we boosted the organization's membership by a whopping 475%. Additionally, I held a position as a Teaching Assistant for the CS180 course and played a mentoring role in numerous CS minority initiatives. At Salesforce, I proactively stepped up as the Accessibility Champion for my team, committing myself to ensure our product consistently caters to individuals of varying capabilities.
